Introduction to Agroforestry

Agroforestry is a land management system that integrates trees and shrubs into agricultural landscapes to improve productivity, biodiversity, and sustainability. By combining forestry and agricultural practices, agroforestry provides multiple benefits, including soil conservation, enhanced biodiversity, and increased resilience to climate change.

Benefits and Practices

Benefits of Agroforestry

  • Improves soil fertility and health
  • Reduces erosion and runoff
  • Enhances biodiversity and wildlife habitat
  • Increases farm resilience to climate change
  • Provides additional income through timber, fruits, or nuts

Common Agroforestry Practices

  • Alley Cropping: Planting rows of trees or shrubs between crops
  • Silvopasture: Integrating trees with pastureland for grazing
  • Forest Farming: Cultivating crops under a forest canopy
  • Windbreaks: Planting trees to reduce wind speed and protect crops
